Interests

Quantum information is an exciting field, bringing together the curious, counter-intuitive properties of quantum mechanics with ideas from computer science such as how to quantify information, and what it means to compute efficiently.

In general, I enjoy collaborating on interesting conceptual ideas where concrete mathematical statements can be made. Some of my current interests and projects include:

  • Quantum Measurements
    •            - which sets of measurements are useful?
  • Resources for Quantum Computation
    •            - which properties of quantum mechanics imply classical speedup?
  • Near-term Quantum Computing
    •            - how can quantum computers provide real value, sooner?

The field is now extremely broad, and I am keen to continue expanding my understanding and ability to contribute to new topics. In particular, I am interested in learning more about complexity theory and fault-tolerant architectures.

Bio
